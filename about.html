I am MSc in Chemical Engineer (2011, University of Santiago de Compostela, Spain). In 2015 I completed my PhD in Applied Biological Sciences (Chemistry and Bioprocess Technology) about microalgae biorefineries, nutrient recovery and bioprocess design. The PhD was developed between the universities of Ghent (Belgium), Twente (The Netherlands) and the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (Germany), and funded by the Fundación Pedro Barrié de la Maza (Spain) and the IWT (Belgium). Between 2017 and 2019, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and research affiliate at the Department of Biomedical Engineering at Tufts University (USA), working on a combined experimental-computational approach to the synthesis of biopolymeric and biobased materials. I am currently a Marie Curie Fellow at DSM working on the engineering of structural proteins for biomedical materials with controllable mechanical and biological properties.
